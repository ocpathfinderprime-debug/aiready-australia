## POST 3: Credibility

We didn't build AIReady Australia by reading about AI. We built it by running it.

Here's what we actually learned:

**The first mistake is buying tools before you've mapped your problems.**
We've seen businesses pay $500/month for AI software they barely use because it sounded good in a demo. Tools don't save time. Well-defined processes do. The tool just runs the process.

**The second mistake is starting with the flashiest use case.**
Chatbots. AI-generated content. Image generation. These get the press. But the biggest wins inside a real business? Automated data handling. Smart email routing. Meeting summaries. Workflow triggers. Boring stuff that runs 24/7 and never calls in sick.

**The third mistake is doing it alone.**
Not because AI is hard — it's not. But because when you're inside a business, you're blind to your own inefficiencies. An outside perspective maps the gaps you've stopped seeing.

We've audited our own operations end-to-end. We know what questions to ask. We know where the time usually hides.

## POST 4: Myth-Busting

AI isn't just for big companies with big budgets. That's outdated thinking.

Here's what a small business owner can actually deploy today for under $100/month:

**ChatGPT / Claude** — Draft emails, write job ads, summarise documents, answer customer FAQs. $20-30/month. No technical setup.

**Zapier or Make** — Connect your existing tools so data moves automatically between them. No code. Costs $20-100/month depending on volume.

**Otter.ai or Fireflies** — Auto-transcribe every meeting. Generate action items. Email them to attendees. ~$20/month.

**Notion AI** — Build a knowledge base your whole team can search. AI finds the answer. Saves hours of "who knows how to do X?" conversations. $10/month.

Total: under $100/month. For a business doing $1M/year, that's 0.1% of revenue. The time savings are typically 20-40x the cost.

The gap between large and small businesses on AI isn't budget. It's awareness. Most small business owners don't know what's available, what's actually useful, or where to start.

That's the gap we close.
