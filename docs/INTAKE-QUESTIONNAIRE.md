# AIReady Australia — Intake Questionnaire

**Version:** 1.0
**Estimated completion time:** 20–35 minutes
**Instructions:** Answer as specifically as you can. The more detail you give, the more specific and useful your audit report will be. There are no wrong answers — we're trying to understand your business as it actually operates today, not as it ideally would.

---

## SECTION A: Business Basics

---

### Question 1

**Question:** What is your business name, industry, and the primary product or service you sell?

**Why we ask:** We calibrate AI opportunity assessments by industry. A tradie business has different high-value AI use cases than a professional services firm or an ecommerce store. Without this, we can't give you specific tool recommendations.

**Answer format:** Text (open field — please be specific, e.g., "residential building and renovation company, Perth WA" not just "construction")

---

### Question 2

**Question:** How many full-time equivalent (FTE) staff do you currently have? Include part-time staff as fractions (e.g., two part-timers = 1 FTE). Include yourself.

**Why we ask:** Team size directly affects which AI tools are appropriate and cost-effective. It also helps us estimate the potential time savings in FTE-equivalent terms, which makes the ROI calculation meaningful.

**Answer format:** Multiple choice
- Just me (solo operator)
- 2–5 FTE
- 6–15 FTE
- 16–30 FTE
- 31–50 FTE
- 51–100 FTE
- 100+ FTE

---

### Question 3

**Question:** What is your approximate annual revenue? This is used only for scoping — it helps us prioritise recommendations that are proportionate to your business size.

**Why we ask:** A $200K/year business and a $5M/year business have very different ROI thresholds for AI investment. We won't recommend a $2,000/month platform to a solo operator.

**Answer format:** Multiple choice
- Under $250K
- $250K–$500K
- $500K–$1M
- $1M–$3M
- $3M–$10M
- $10M–$30M
- Over $30M
- Prefer not to say

---

### Question 4

**Question:** Where is your business located, and who are your customers? (e.g., "Brisbane-based, serving clients nationally" or "Melbourne retail, local only")

**Why we ask:** Location affects tool recommendations (Australian data sovereignty requirements, local integrations) and customer profile affects which AI applications are most relevant — B2B vs. B2C workflows look very different.

**Answer format:** Text (open field)

---

### Question 5

**Question:** How long has the business been operating, and what stage would you describe it as?

**Why we ask:** Business maturity shapes AI readiness. An early-stage business often needs to build data infrastructure before AI can help. A mature business often has data already — just locked in siloed systems.

**Answer format:** Multiple choice (select stage, then add years in text field)
- Startup / Pre-revenue (under 2 years)
- Early growth (2–5 years, still finding product-market fit)
- Established growth (5–10 years, scaling what works)
- Mature / optimising (10+ years, focused on efficiency)
- Enterprise / market leader (dominant position, transformation focus)

---

## SECTION B: Technology Stack

---

### Question 6

**Question:** List every software tool or platform your business currently uses. Include everything — accounting, CRM, scheduling, email, project management, ecommerce, communication, industry-specific tools. Don't filter — even "just Excel" counts.

**Why we ask:** This is one of the most important questions in the audit. We can only identify integration opportunities, redundancies, and automation potential if we know what you're already running. Many of the best AI wins come from tools the business already pays for but underuses.

**Answer format:** Text (open field — list format preferred, e.g., "Xero, HubSpot, Slack, Microsoft 365, Shopify, ServiceM8")

---

### Question 7

**Question:** Of those tools, which ones does your team actually use daily? Which are used rarely or barely at all?

**Why we ask:** There's often a gap between what's installed and what's actually embedded in workflows. A CRM with half the team not logging in is a different problem than a CRM with daily usage. This helps us separate live infrastructure from shelfware.

**Answer format:** Text (open field — e.g., "Daily: Xero, Gmail, Slack. Rarely used: HubSpot, we bought it but it's not adopted")

---

### Question 8

**Question:** How would you rate your team's overall comfort with technology and new software?

**Why we ask:** Implementation readiness is as important as opportunity identification. We won't recommend complex AI tools to a team that struggles with basic software adoption. This calibrates the difficulty level of our recommendations.

**Answer format:** Scale 1–10
- 1–2: Most staff struggle with basic software; any new tool requires heavy training
- 3–4: Functional but cautious; adoption is slow and often incomplete
- 5–6: Average — most tools get used, some adoption gaps
- 7–8: Comfortable; team picks up new tools reasonably quickly
- 9–10: Tech-forward; team actively seeks better tools and adopts them fast

---

### Question 9

**Question:** Do you currently use any AI tools in your business? If yes, list them and briefly describe how you're using them.

**Why we ask:** Businesses already using AI (even informally — like ChatGPT for email drafts) have a higher implementation baseline. We build on what's already there rather than starting from scratch. If you're not using any, that's fine — we'll start with fundamentals.

**Answer format:** Text (open field — e.g., "Yes — we use ChatGPT occasionally for writing but nothing systematic. We tried an AI chatbot on the website but removed it.")

---

## SECTION C: Bottlenecks & Pain Points

---

### Question 10

**Question:** What are the top 3 things in your business that take up too much time, cost too much, or frustrate you most? Be as specific as possible — not "admin is a problem" but "we spend 3 hours every Monday manually doing X."

**Why we ask:** This is the most direct path to high-value findings. When customers describe specific pain points in concrete terms, we can usually identify a direct AI solution and estimate the time/cost savings with reasonable accuracy.

**Answer format:** Text (open field — please describe all three, one paragraph each if possible)

---

### Question 11

**Question:** Where do things most commonly fall through the cracks in your business? (E.g., leads not followed up, invoices sent late, jobs not properly documented, customer complaints not tracked.)

**Why we ask:** "Things falling through the cracks" is usually a workflow gap that AI can close. These are often the highest-ROI opportunities because they represent direct revenue loss or customer churn, not just inefficiency.

**Answer format:** Text (open field)

---

### Question 12

**Question:** Is there any task in your business that you know is important but consistently doesn't get done because no one has time? What is it?

**Why we ask:** Chronic neglect of important tasks is a strong signal for AI opportunity. These are usually tasks where human attention is the bottleneck — content creation, follow-up emails, reporting, documentation — that AI can either do autonomously or dramatically accelerate.

**Answer format:** Text (open field)

---

## SECTION D: Workflows

---

### Question 13

**Question:** Walk us through what happens from the moment a new lead or inquiry comes in until they become a customer. Who does what, using which tools, and roughly how long does each step take?

**Why we ask:** The lead-to-customer journey is one of the highest-leverage areas for AI. Speed, personalisation, and follow-up consistency all affect conversion rates. We need the actual current-state process, not the ideal one.

**Answer format:** Text (open field — step-by-step is ideal, e.g., "1. Lead comes in via web form → 2. Goes to shared Gmail inbox → 3. Someone calls them within a day or two → 4. We send a quote in Word → 5. Follow up by phone if no reply")

---

### Question 14

**Question:** Describe your customer service workflow. How do customers contact you for support, how are requests tracked, and how are they resolved?

**Why we ask:** Customer service is consistently one of the highest-volume areas for AI assistance. Understanding the channel mix, inquiry types, and resolution process helps us identify what proportion of contacts could be handled automatically vs. need human judgment.

**Answer format:** Text (open field — include: channels customers use, average weekly volume if known, how it's tracked, who handles it)

---

### Question 15

**Question:** How does your marketing currently work? Who creates content, how often, on which platforms, and what's your approximate monthly spend on marketing (including staff time)?

**Why we ask:** AI content tools can dramatically reduce marketing production costs and increase output volume. But the right approach depends entirely on current state — a business with no marketing needs a different recommendation than one with an active but expensive agency relationship.

**Answer format:** Text (open field — include channels, frequency, who does the work, rough monthly cost)

---

### Question 16

**Question:** How is invoicing, payments, and financial reporting currently handled? Who does it, how often, using which tools, and roughly how many hours per week does it take across the team?

**Why we ask:** Finance workflows are often the easiest area to automate because the data is already digital and the tasks are highly repetitive. Understanding the current workflow lets us identify whether the opportunity is in the tools (better software) or the process (better rules and automation within existing tools).

**Answer format:** Text (open field)

---

## SECTION E: Data Availability & Quality

---

### Question 17

**Question:** Where does your customer data live? Is it in a CRM, spreadsheet, accounting software, or somewhere else? Roughly how complete and up-to-date is it?

**Why we ask:** AI is only as good as the data it has to work with. A business with clean, centralised customer data can deploy personalisation and automation tools quickly. A business with scattered, incomplete data needs data infrastructure work first — and we'll say so clearly.

**Answer format:** Text + scale
- Text: Where it lives
- Scale 1–10 for data quality: 1 = scattered/unreliable, 10 = clean, centralised, up to date

---

### Question 18

**Question:** Do you have documented processes (SOPs, checklists, training guides) for your key workflows? If yes, where are they stored and how current are they?

**Why we ask:** AI tools like internal chatbots, onboarding assistants, and knowledge bases need existing documentation to train on. A business with good SOPs can deploy these quickly. A business with no documentation needs to create it first — which itself can be AI-assisted.

**Answer format:** Multiple choice + text
- No documented processes — it's all in people's heads
- Some processes documented, but inconsistent and possibly outdated
- Most key processes documented, stored in [where?]
- Fully documented, maintained, and accessible to the team

---

## SECTION F: Budget & Implementation Capacity

---

### Question 19

**Question:** What is your approximate monthly budget available for new AI tools or software subscriptions? This is separate from implementation costs — just the ongoing tool spend.

**Why we ask:** Tool recommendations are worthless if they're outside budget. We filter every recommendation through your stated budget. There's no shame in a tight budget — plenty of the best AI wins cost under $100/month.

**Answer format:** Multiple choice
- $0 — I need free tools only
- $1–$100/month
- $101–$300/month
- $301–$500/month
- $501–$1,000/month
- $1,001–$3,000/month
- $3,000+/month

---

### Question 20

**Question:** Who in your business would be responsible for implementing AI changes? Do they have technical skills, time, and decision-making authority? Or would you need external help?

**Why we ask:** The best recommendation is one that will actually get done. If implementation depends on a part-time bookkeeper who's already overloaded, we'll note that constraint and recommend staged rollouts or simpler tools. If you have a technically capable operations person with bandwidth, we'll aim higher.

**Answer format:** Text (open field — describe the person/team, their skills, their current capacity, and whether they'd need vendor support)

---

## SECTION G: Goals & Priorities

---

### Question 21

**Question:** What is the single most important outcome you want from this audit? What would make you consider this $497/$997/$1,997 well spent?

**Why we ask:** Different owners want different things from an audit. Some want a list of quick wins to reduce their workload this month. Some want a 12-month AI strategy. Some want to know if they're falling behind competitors. Understanding your primary goal shapes how we present findings and what we emphasise.

**Answer format:** Text (open field — be honest, one paragraph)

---

### Question 22

**Question:** In the next 12 months, what are the top 2–3 business goals you're working toward? (E.g., grow revenue by 30%, reduce staff costs, expand to a new location, improve customer retention, sell the business.)

**Why we ask:** AI recommendations that align with your strategic goals are worth 10x more than technically sound recommendations that don't. A business planning to sell in 12 months needs a very different AI strategy than one planning to double headcount.

**Answer format:** Text (open field — list 2–3 goals with brief context)

---

### Question 23

**Question:** Are there areas of the business where you would NOT want AI involved, for any reason? (Privacy, culture, customer relationship concerns, anything.)

**Why we ask:** Respecting constraints is part of a good recommendation. If you don't want AI in customer-facing communication because your brand is built on personal relationships, we'll focus elsewhere. If you have privacy concerns about a certain data set, we'll note it. These are legitimate considerations, not obstacles.

**Answer format:** Text (open field — e.g., "We don't want AI speaking directly to customers. We're fine with AI in back-office work.")

---

## SECTION H: Previous AI/Automation Experience

---

### Question 24

**Question:** Have you previously tried to automate or use AI in your business? If yes, describe what you tried, what happened, and why it did or didn't work out.

**Why we ask:** Past attempts reveal a lot. Failed implementations often tell us more about the business than successful ones — they reveal technical constraints, change management gaps, or vendor mismatches. We use this to avoid recommending something you've already tried and rejected.

**Answer format:** Text (open field — e.g., "Yes — we tried a chatbot 2 years ago. It couldn't answer specific questions and customers hated it. We turned it off after 3 months. The bot wasn't trained properly and we didn't have the in-house skills to maintain it.")

---

### Question 25

**Question:** Is there anything else about your business, your situation, or your concerns about AI that you think we should know before we start the audit? Anything that didn't fit neatly into the questions above?

**Why we ask:** Intake questionnaires never capture everything. This open field is intentionally broad. Some of the most useful information in an audit comes from what clients mention here — a competitor that just automated their whole sales team, a key staff member who's leaving, a planned acquisition, a regulatory constraint. If it feels relevant, write it down.

**Answer format:** Text (open field — no length limit, anything goes)

## Internal Notes (Not Customer-Facing)

**Auditor checklist before starting the report:**
- [ ] All 25 questions answered or noted as skipped
- [ ] Q10 (top 3 pain points) has specific, concrete examples
- [ ] Q6 (tech stack) is complete enough to map integrations
- [ ] Q17 (data quality) score is noted — if below 5, flag in Implementation Readiness section
- [ ] Q19 (budget) is noted — filter all tool recommendations through this
- [ ] Q20 (implementation capacity) is assessed — if weak, recommend staged approach
- [ ] Q21 (primary goal) is incorporated into Executive Summary framing
- [ ] Q24 (past failures) — cross-reference against your recommendations

**If critical fields are missing:** Send one targeted clarification email before beginning. Ask only for what you need. Do not re-send the whole questionnaire.
