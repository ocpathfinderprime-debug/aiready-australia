## What The Customer Receives

Every AIReady Australia audit produces a single, structured PDF report called the **AI Readiness Report**. It runs 18–35 pages depending on tier, and reads like a business document — not an academic paper or a tech manual.

### Report Structure

**Cover Page**
Business name, date, tier, auditor reference number.

**Executive Summary (1 page)**
Three paragraphs. What the business is, what we found, and what we recommend doing first. Written for the owner, not the IT department. Jargon-free.

**Readiness Score Dashboard**
A visual one-page overview showing the business's score across all 8 audit categories (0–100 per category, plus an overall AIReady Score). Colour-coded: red/amber/green. The owner sees at a glance where they're strong and where they're losing money to manual work.

**Category Findings (8 sections)**
For each of the 8 AI opportunity categories, the report includes:
- Current state summary (what we observed)
- Gap analysis (where AI could replace or reduce manual effort)
- Specific opportunity (the actual thing they could automate or improve)
- Estimated effort to implement (low/medium/high)
- Estimated impact (hours saved per week, or revenue potential, or risk reduced)

**Priority Action Plan**
Three tiers of recommendation:
- **Do Now** — quick wins, low cost, high impact. Usually 2–3 items.
- **Do Next** — medium-term opportunities that require some setup or budget.
- **Do Later** — longer-horizon moves that depend on earlier wins.

Each item includes: what to do, why it matters, approximate cost range (AUD), and suggested next step.

**Tool & Vendor Recommendations**
A curated shortlist of AI tools relevant to their specific situation. Not a generic list — filtered to their industry, budget, and technical maturity. Each tool gets a one-paragraph explanation of why it fits them and what to watch out for.

**Implementation Readiness Assessment**
An honest rating of the business's capacity to implement AI changes, covering: technical capability of staff, data quality, budget availability, and change-readiness. This section prevents recommendations from landing in the too-hard basket.

**Appendix**
Raw intake responses for reference, scoring methodology, and glossary of terms used in the report.

### The Value It Delivers

The report tells a business owner three things they couldn't easily find out on their own:
1. Exactly where AI could save them time or money right now
2. Which tools are worth buying and which are hype
3. What to do first, in what order, and roughly what it'll cost

It replaces 20+ hours of self-research, vendor pitches, and YouTube rabbit holes. It's specific to their business — not a template dressed up as personalised advice.

## The Audit Delivery Process

### Stage 1: Purchase (Day 0)

Customer selects tier and pays via checkout page (Stripe). Immediately receives:
- Confirmation email with order reference
- Link to the intake questionnaire (hosted form)
- Welcome video (3 minutes) explaining what to expect and how to get the most from their answers
- Calendar link to book their debrief call (Business and Enterprise tiers only)

**Automation note:** Purchase triggers intake form delivery and CRM entry automatically.

---

### Stage 2: Intake (Days 1–2)

Customer completes the 25-question intake questionnaire. Form is designed to take 20–35 minutes. If incomplete after 48 hours, one automated follow-up nudge is sent.

Once the form is submitted:
- Customer receives an acknowledgement email confirming receipt and timeline
- Intake responses are compiled into a structured brief for the auditor
- Auditor (or AI-assisted audit pipeline) is assigned the brief

**Quality gate:** If any critical field is missing or ambiguous, the auditor sends one targeted clarification email before proceeding.

---

### Stage 3: Audit (Days 2–6 depending on tier)

The auditor works through the 8 categories using the intake responses as raw material. For each category:
1. Review what the customer said about current state
2. Score against our readiness rubric (0–100)
3. Identify specific, named opportunities (not generic observations)
4. Draft findings using the report template
5. Select relevant tools from the curated library
6. Write prioritised recommendations

**Starter:** AI-assisted draft with human review and final edit
**Business:** Human-led audit with AI assistance for research and formatting
**Enterprise:** Senior auditor lead, full human production, peer review before delivery

---

### Stage 4: Report Production (Days 5–9)

Draft report is assembled into the PDF template. Quality checklist applied:
- All 8 categories scored and populated
- At least 3 specific opportunities named (not generic)
- Tool recommendations match their industry and budget
- Priority action plan is sequenced and realistic
- Executive summary written in plain English
- No placeholder text, no template artifacts

Report reviewed by a second auditor before delivery (Business and Enterprise).

---

### Stage 5: Delivery (Day 7–10)

Customer receives an email with:
- PDF report attached (plus secure download link)
- For Starter: link to 30-minute recorded video walkthrough
- For Business/Enterprise: calendar confirmation of debrief call
- Next steps summary (3 bullet points)
- Referral offer: "Know another business owner who'd benefit? Forward this link."

---

### Stage 6: Debrief Call (Business tier: within 5 days post-delivery / Enterprise: within 3 days)

45–60 minute live call with the auditor. Agenda:
1. Quick tour of the report (10 min)
2. Customer questions on findings (15–20 min)
3. Deep dive on top 2 priorities (10–15 min)
4. Discuss implementation pathway options (5–10 min)
5. Next steps and close (5 min)

Call recorded and shared with customer.

---

### Stage 7: Follow-up & Retention (Days 14–90)

- **Day 14:** Check-in email: "How's it landing? Any questions?" No sales pitch.
- **Day 30:** Follow-up check-in call (Business and Enterprise). 15–20 minutes. Focus: have they started implementing? What's blocking them?
- **Day 60:** Starter tier check-in email. Business/Enterprise: review progress, note what's been implemented.
- **Day 90:** Enterprise 90-day check-in call. Review ROI on anything implemented.

At each touchpoint: light offer to extend into implementation support retainer. Never pushy — just available.

## AI Opportunity Categories Assessed (8 Categories)

---

### Category 1: Administrative Operations

**Description:** The day-to-day back-office work that keeps a business running but adds no direct value — scheduling, data entry, document handling, internal communications, filing.

**What we look for:**
- How many hours per week are spent on manual data entry?
- Are there recurring document types (quotes, contracts, invoices, reports) created manually?
- Is scheduling or appointment management handled by a human?
- Are internal emails or messages written from scratch repeatedly?

**Example finding:**
*"Your admin team spends approximately 6 hours per week manually copying customer data from email inquiries into your CRM. An AI-assisted data extraction tool (e.g., Zapier + GPT-4 integration or Make.com) could reduce this to under 30 minutes with 90%+ accuracy. Estimated annual saving: 280 hours, or ~$8,400 at your current admin wage."*

---

### Category 2: Customer Service & Support

**Description:** How the business handles inbound customer inquiries, complaints, follow-ups, and support requests — and whether AI could handle a meaningful portion of that volume.

**What we look for:**
- Volume and type of inbound inquiries (email, phone, live chat, social)
- What percentage of inquiries are repetitive vs. complex?
- Is there a FAQ or knowledge base already in place?
- Average response time and first-response SLA

**Example finding:**
*"Approximately 65% of your customer inquiries are variations of the same 12 questions (shipping times, returns policy, product availability). An AI chatbot trained on your existing knowledge base could handle these without human involvement, reducing your customer service team's inbound load by an estimated 10 hours per week."*

---

### Category 3: Sales & Lead Management

**Description:** The sales pipeline — lead capture, qualification, follow-up, proposal generation, and conversion. AI can compress timeframes and remove human bottlenecks at every step.

**What we look for:**
- Lead sources and volume
- Current CRM usage and data quality
- How leads are qualified and followed up
- Time from inquiry to proposal/quote
- Percentage of leads that go cold due to slow follow-up

**Example finding:**
*"Your sales team takes an average of 3–5 days to send a quote after initial inquiry. Competitors in your category average under 24 hours. An AI-assisted quoting tool integrated with your CRM could cut this to under 2 hours, without increasing headcount. Based on your stated close rate, faster response could recover 2–3 lost deals per month."*

---

### Category 4: Marketing & Content

**Description:** How the business creates, distributes, and measures marketing content — and where AI can accelerate output, reduce cost, or improve targeting.

**What we look for:**
- Current content types (social, email, blog, video, ads)
- How content is created (in-house, agency, freelancer)
- Email marketing frequency and tooling
- SEO and content strategy maturity
- Ad spend and creative production workflow

**Example finding:**
*"You're currently outsourcing social media content to a freelancer at $1,200/month for 12 posts. With AI content tools (e.g., Jasper or Claude API with brand training), you could produce the same volume in-house for under $100/month in tool costs, freeing the $1,100 difference and giving your team direct control over messaging."*

---

### Category 5: Financial Management & Reporting

**Description:** How the business handles bookkeeping, invoicing, cash flow tracking, and financial reporting — and where manual effort, errors, or delays create risk.

**What we look for:**
- Current accounting software and level of automation
- How invoices are created and tracked
- How financial reports are generated and how often
- Manual reconciliation workload
- Cash flow visibility

**Example finding:**
*"Your bookkeeper manually reconciles bank transactions weekly, taking approximately 4 hours. Your accounting platform (Xero) supports AI-powered bank rules and auto-categorisation that could reduce this to under 30 minutes with one-time setup. This is a zero-cost opportunity — the feature is already in your subscription."*

---

### Category 6: HR, Recruitment & Onboarding

**Description:** How the business finds, hires, and onboards staff — and whether AI can reduce the time-to-hire, improve candidate screening, or systematise onboarding.

**What we look for:**
- Frequency of hiring and current recruitment process
- How job ads are written and where they're posted
- How applications are screened
- Onboarding documentation and process
- Staff training and knowledge transfer methods

**Example finding:**
*"Your onboarding process is currently undocumented — new hires shadow existing staff for 2 weeks. This creates dependency on individual employees and inconsistent training outcomes. An AI-assisted SOP builder could help you document and standardise onboarding in a two-day sprint, reducing new hire ramp time and protecting institutional knowledge."*

---

### Category 7: Operations & Supply Chain

**Description:** The physical or logistical workflows that deliver your product or service — inventory, scheduling, supplier management, fulfilment, quality control.

**What we look for:**
- Inventory management method (manual, spreadsheet, software)
- Demand forecasting approach
- Supplier communication workflow
- Job scheduling and resource allocation
- Quality control processes and defect tracking

**Example finding:**
*"You're managing stock levels via a spreadsheet updated manually every Monday. You've had three stockout events in the past six months, each resulting in delayed orders and customer complaints. An AI-assisted inventory tool (e.g., Cin7 with demand forecasting) could predict stockouts 2–3 weeks in advance based on sales velocity, preventing these events. Setup cost: ~$300/month, estimated loss-prevention value: $15,000–$40,000/year based on your stated margin."*

---

### Category 8: Data, Analytics & Decision-Making

**Description:** How the business collects, stores, and uses data to make decisions — and whether AI can surface insights that are currently buried in spreadsheets, siloed systems, or never captured at all.

**What we look for:**
- What data is currently collected and where it lives
- How business decisions are currently made (gut, spreadsheet, dashboard)
- Whether data is siloed across disconnected systems
- Reporting frequency and who consumes reports
- Any existing analytics tools in use

**Example finding:**
*"Your sales data, customer data, and financial data live in three separate systems that don't talk to each other. You're making pricing and inventory decisions without a consolidated view. A simple BI integration (e.g., Google Looker Studio connected to your three platforms) could give you a real-time dashboard in under a week, at no additional software cost."*
