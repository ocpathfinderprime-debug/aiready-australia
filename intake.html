Tell Us About Your Business

Your answers drive everything in your audit. The more specific you are, the sharper the output. There are no wrong answers — we're mapping how your business actually runs today.

A

Business Basics

Help us understand who you are and the shape of your business.

Be specific — e.g. "residential building and renovation company, Perth WA" not just "construction".

Include part-time staff as fractions (two part-timers = 1 FTE). Include yourself.

Used for scoping only — helps us recommend tools proportionate to your business size.

e.g. "Brisbane-based, serving clients nationally" or "Melbourne retail, local only".

Select the best fit, then add years of operation in the field below.

B

Technology Stack

What tools are you running? We'll find integration and automation opportunities here.

Include everything — accounting, CRM, scheduling, email, project management, ecommerce, comms, industry-specific tools. Even "just Excel" counts. List format preferred.

e.g. "Daily: Xero, Gmail, Slack. Rarely used: HubSpot — we bought it but it's not adopted."

1 = most staff struggle with basic software · 10 = tech-forward, team actively seeks better tools.

1 — Struggles with basics 10 — Tech-forward
5 / 10

Average — most tools get used, some adoption gaps

If not, that's fine — we'll start from fundamentals. Be honest about informal use too (e.g. "ChatGPT for emails").

C

Bottlenecks & Pain Points

Where is your business bleeding time, money, or energy?

Be concrete — not "admin is a problem" but "we spend 3 hours every Monday manually doing X." One paragraph per item if possible.

e.g. leads not followed up, invoices sent late, jobs not properly documented, customer complaints not tracked.

Chronic neglect of important tasks is a strong AI signal — content creation, follow-ups, reporting, documentation.

D

Workflows

Walk us through how your business actually operates day to day.

Who does what, which tools they use, and roughly how long each step takes. We need the actual current process, not the ideal one.

How do customers contact you, how are requests tracked, and how are they resolved? Include channels, weekly volume if known, and who handles it.

Who creates content, how often, on which platforms, and your approximate monthly spend (including staff time).

Who does it, how often, which tools, and roughly how many hours per week across the team.

E

Data Availability & Quality

AI is only as good as your data. Let's find out what you're working with.

Is it in a CRM, spreadsheet, accounting software, or scattered? Rate the quality from 1 (scattered/unreliable) to 10 (clean, centralised, up to date).

1 — Scattered / unreliable 10 — Clean & centralised
5 / 10

Moderate — some good data, significant gaps

Where are they stored and how current are they?

F

Budget & Implementation Capacity

Every recommendation gets filtered through what's realistic for your situation.

This is separate from implementation costs — just ongoing tool spend. No shame in a tight budget.

Describe the person/team — their skills, current capacity, and whether they'd need vendor support.

G

Goals & Priorities

What success looks like — and what areas you want to leave alone.

What would make this money well spent? Quick wins to reduce workload? A 12-month AI strategy? Knowing if you're falling behind competitors? Be honest.

e.g. grow revenue by 30%, reduce staff costs, expand to a new location, improve customer retention, sell the business.

Privacy, culture, customer relationship concerns — anything. These are legitimate constraints, not obstacles.

H

Previous AI / Automation Experience

What's been tried, what worked, what didn't — and anything else we should know.

If yes: what you tried, what happened, and why it did or didn't work. Past failures tell us more than successes.

Anything that didn't fit the questions above — a competitor that just automated their sales team, a key staff member leaving, a planned acquisition, a regulatory constraint. If it feels relevant, write it down. No length limit.
